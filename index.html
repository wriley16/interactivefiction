Okay, let's go back to 1936.

Konrad Zuse released the Z1 Computer, the first freely programmable computer.


1951: UNIVAC When the Univac was invented, it consisted of magnetic tape with a speed of 12,800 characters per second. It faced competition from IBM's computing equipment too.


1954. FORTRAN It was the first high level programming lamguage and was commercialy released in 1957. Fortran is still used today for programming scientififc and mathematical applications.


1958. Integrated Circuits 1961 saw the first commercially available integrated circuits. "What we didn't realise then was that integrated circuits would reduce the cost of electric function by a factor of a million to one, nothing had ever done that for anything tomorrow." -Jack Kilby


1969. ARPAnet was invented. This was the precursor to the Internet we know today and was developped by the american army. Under this, email and the abilty to send messages was created.


1981. MS-DOS. IBM introduced a 'revolution in a box'. Bill Gates wrote this brilliant new Disk Operating System which did indeed revolutionize computing.


1984. Could we expect anything less from a company that was now being run by the former president of Pepsi-Cola? The company under Steve Jobs's vision revealed a revolutionary product that is still one of its kind: the Mac.
